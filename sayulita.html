The Trendiest Mexican Beach in 2024: Sayulita

Welcome to Sayulita!

Famous for its beaches, surfing, and laid-back ambiance, Sayulita is the perfect destination if you're looking to get off the beaten path. This town is a haven for expat's and digital nomads due to its bohemian feel and relaxed atmosphere. Sayulita offers an abundance of colorful streets, art galleries, and eateries, creating one of Mexico's best hidden gems. In this guide, we’ll be giving you the best way to see Sayulita and experience its true authenticity.

Sayulita is a small coastal town located on the west coast of Mexico, in the state of Nayarit. It is a popular tourist destination known for its laid-back atmosphere, beautiful beaches, surfing opportunities, and vibrant local culture. Sayulita is situated about 40 kilometers (25 miles) north of Puerto Vallarta, a major resort city.


Getting to Sayulita

Unless you are driving down through Mexico, the most common way to reach Sayulita is byway of Puerto Vallarta International Airport (PVR). Puerto Vallarta offers the most convenient airport and is only a 45 minute drive from Sayulita. Flights into PVR run daily and are available for a fraction of the price when compared to neighboring airports. From the airport, you will take a 45 minute drive north to Sayulita. If you aren't renting a car, taxis and Uber are available, as well as shuttles for select hotels in the area.

Things to Do


Avenida Revolucion

Avenida Revolución is a historic and bustling street in the city center known for its markets, shops, restaurants, bars, and vibrant atmosphere. It has been a significant tourist and commercial area in Tijuana for many years.

Sayulita Plaza

Sayulita Plaza is a gathering area located in the heart of Sayulita. Sayulita Plaza is a focal point of the town and serves as a hub for various activities and events. The plaza often hosts artisan markets, live music performances, cultural events, and festivals, making it an integral part of the Sayulita's cultural and social life.

Mercado de Artesanias Sayulita

Mercado de Artesanias Sayulita is a traditional market known for authentic Mexican goods. This market is a perfect place to grab a souvenir or a bite to eat. Hours vary, but the market is generally open on weekends.

Monkey Mountain: Patzcuaro Trailhead

Monkey Mountain was my favorite hike in the area and can be reached by car or taxi. For a full guide on Monkey Mountain, please see the link here.

Beaches in Sayulita

Playa De Los Muertos and Playa Las Cuevas are my two favorite beaches in Sayulita. Both are more low-key and less crowded than Playa Sayulita (which is located in the heart of town). Playa De Los Muertos is about a 10 minute walk from town and Playa Las Cuevas is about a 45 minute walk from town.

Places to Eat


Don Pedro's

Don Pedro's is my favorite spot in town. I visited Don Pedro's on my first day in Sayulita and I found its ambience hard to beat. There was live music and people dancing, creating a welcoming entrance upon arrival. We were sat at a table in the sand and watched one of the best sunsets I've ever experienced. The food is the definition of authentic. A must-try from Don Pedro's is their assortment of seafood tostadas.

Tierra Viva

Tierra Viva is a traditional restaurant that offers a wide selection of food for an affordable price. This spot is known for its authentic Mexican cuisine and wide variety of tequila. Group tequila tastings are available and take place on the terrace of the restaurant.

Paris Delights

Paris Delights is your typical Parisian cafe located in Sayulita known for their large assortment of traditional treats and pastries. My personal favorite from this spot is the chocolate almond croissant.

Northside Cafe

Northside Cafe is a perfect spot if you are looking for a quick, easy, and affordable breakfast. This spot is known for its large presence of expats, and serves as a hotspot for locals and visitors alike. Northside offers a large menu of breakfast favorites.

Rollado Ice Cream

Rollado is an amazing ice cream spot located in their iconic pink truck. The ice cream is rolled fresh and made-to-order. Their iconic "ice cream tacos" are rolled ice cream served in a waffle cone, and are a must try.

Where to Stay


Los Almendros

Los Almendros is where I stayed during my time in Sayulita and I was pleasantly surprised upon arrival. For more information on Los Almendros, please see the link below.

Hotelity Los Suenos

A tranquil yoga-focused hotel, Hotelito Los Sueños is perfect for those seeking relaxation and rejuvenation. Located a short walk from the beach, it offers cozy rooms, daily yoga classes, and a peaceful rooftop terrace where guests can unwind in the tropical atmosphere.

Villa Amor

Villa Amor is a stunning boutique hotel perched on a cliffside, providing breathtaking ocean views. Its individually designed villas are perfect for a luxurious and private stay. With its unique architecture and lush gardens, Villa Amor delivers an upscale, authentic Mexican experience.

Petit Hotel Hafa

Petit Hotel Hafa offers a bohemian, artistic vibe in the heart of Sayulita. Its charming and colorful decor, combined with personalized hospitality, creates a cozy and intimate atmosphere. Just steps from the beach, the hotel’s rooftop terrace is perfect for enjoying sunsets and ocean breezes.

Casa Love

Casa Love is a boutique beachfront hotel with a laid-back, eclectic style. Its simple yet artistic decor reflects Sayulita’s creative spirit. With direct beach access, it’s an ideal spot for surfers and beach lovers. Guests can relax in the communal areas or explore nearby shops and cafes.
